    • Kelly Parker is a Detroit born, Los Angeles based, award winning documentary filmmaker who develops and produces feature length documentaries and broadcast documentary series. Most recently, Game Girls, which premiered in documentary competition at the Berlin International Film Festival in 2018, took home the Jury Grand Prize from Festival International du Film Ind√©pendant de Bordeaux.

    Kelly also recently developed original documentary series' for Link TV and KCET public television, which earned her five L.A. Emmy Awards. The Migrant Kitchen, a character driven documentary food series that she launched in 2016 won Kelly Emmy awards for Season 1 and Season 2 and was nominated for two James Beard Awards. In 2017, Parker co-created and launched the documentary, City Rising, about development and displacement across California, which won three Emmy awards, including Best Social Issue Film. In addition, City Rising won the LA Press Club Journalism Award for Best Documentary and was nominated for an Imagen Award in 2018. Kelly also earned Emmy's for her work on episodes of Artbound and Lost LA, KCET's flagship history series.

    Kelly's experience and storytelling skills have been strengthened by working in several key roles across multiple disciplines including fine art, fiction film, journalism and advertising. In 2013 Kelly joined the Affordable Healthcare Act advertising team as Senior Producer for the campaign launch of Covered California.. Kelly spent 2012 in New York editing two films called, Family Games and Wakaliwood: The Documentary, produced by Ben Barenholtz (Millers Crossing, Requiem for a Dream). Kelly was the cinematographer on Travis Wilkerson's award winning documentary, Distinguished Flying Cross, in 2009 and was awarded a commission as an artist, to produce and direct a video piece for a ground-breaking exhibition, Shrinking Cities, which investigated the state of post-industrial urban regions in 2004.

    Kelly received an MFA from California Institute of the Arts in 2008 where she worked closely with Thom Andersen (Los Angeles Plays Itself, 2003) on her thesis film, a feature length documentary titled South Main, which premiered at the Berlinale International Film Festival in 2008 and screened at several festivals including BFI London where she was invited to speak on a panel titled, "Indiewood is dead, long live the new true Indies," alongside directors Barry Jenkins, Joe Swanberg, Rahmin Bahrani and Azazel Jacobs. South Main was shortlisted for a Gotham Award in 2009 and listed in Filmmaker Magazine as one of Director James Ponsoldt's top ten films of the year. Variety wrote of her directing on South Main, that, "[South Main] marbles furious rigor with streaks of clear-eyed compassion."

    Kelly's work has also been exhibited and supported by festivals, galleries and institutions such as IndieLisboa, Sheffield Docs, Los Angeles Film Forum, Seoul International Film Festival, Rio de Janeiro Film Festival, ARTE, Los Angeles Contemporary Exhibitions, Pratt Manhattan Gallery, The Kunste-Werke Institute for Contemporary Art in Berlin, the Pro Arte Institute St. Petersburgh, IDA, Eurimages, MEDIA Programme of the European Union, Digital Hollywood, Detroit Artists Market Gallery and Detroit Docs, amongst others.
